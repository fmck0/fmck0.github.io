Franco's Burek Recipe (Bootstrap Version)

History

Burek is a pastry that originated in Istanbul in 1500 CE during the early Ottoman Empire. It spread across Europe and influenced pastries in Bosnia, Serbia, and Croatia.

Description of the Recipe

Burek is a flaky pastry filled with minced meat, cheese, or spinach. It is made by layering thin sheets of phyllo dough, adding filling, rolling or spiraling the dough, brushing with oil or butter, and baking until golden brown.

Ingredients

  • Phyllo dough
  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • Salt and pepper
  • Oil or melted butter

Directions

  1. Sauté the meat and onions until browned.
  2. Season with salt and pepper.
  3. Layer phyllo sheets, brushing each with oil or butter.
  4. Add filling and roll or spiral the dough.
  5. Bake at 375°F until golden brown.
