Franco's Burek Recipe (HTML Version)

Burek is a traditional pastry that originated in Istanbul around 1500 CE during the early Ottoman Empire. It later spread across Eastern Europe and became a staple food in countries like Bosnia, Serbia, and Croatia.

Description

Burek is a flaky, buttery pastry filled with minced meat, cheese, or spinach. It is made by layering thin sheets of phyllo dough, adding filling, rolling or spiraling the dough, and baking until golden brown.

Ingredients

  • Phyllo dough
  • 1 lb ground beef or lamb
  • 1 medium onion (finely chopped)
  • 2 tbsp oil or butter
  • Salt and pepper
  • Optional: paprika, parsley

Directions

  1. Sauté chopped onions in oil until soft.
  2. Add ground meat and cook until browned.
  3. Season with salt, pepper, and optional spices.
  4. Layer phyllo sheets, brushing each with oil or butter.
  5. Add filling and roll into a log or spiral.
  6. Bake at 375°F for 30–40 minutes until golden.
